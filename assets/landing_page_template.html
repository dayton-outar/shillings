Understand the Metrics That Matter

We break down the essential concepts used by professional advisors to evaluate stocks on the Jamaica Stock Exchange.

P/E
Price-to-Earnings Ratio
SEP: 13.46x • CCC: 12.93x

Tells you how much investors are paying for each dollar of profit.

P/E = Share Price ÷ Earnings Per Share
Interpretation:
  • High P/E → expected future growth, or possibly expensive.
  • Low P/E → may be undervalued, or weaker growth outlook.
P/B
Price-to-Book Ratio
TJH: 6.46x

Compares market price to the company’s accounting net worth (book value).

P/B = Market Price per Share ÷ Book Value per Share
Interpretation:
  • P/B near 1 → market value aligns with accounting value.
  • High P/B → investors expect strong future returns on assets.

TJH’s high P/B reflects stable long-term cash flows from infrastructure assets like toll roads.

Div. Yield
Dividend Yield
TJH: 3.73%

Measures the income return you receive from dividends relative to the share price.

Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend per Share ÷ Share Price) × 100%
Interpretation:
  • Higher yield → more income per dollar invested.
  • Sustainable yield depends on earnings and payout policy.
Ticker
Stock Ticker & Price
SEP = Seprod Ltd • Current price: $81.73

A ticker is the short trading symbol on the exchange. The share price is the current market value of one share.

Why it matters:
  • Tickers help you quickly identify companies.
  • Price movements reflect market sentiment and fundamentals.
