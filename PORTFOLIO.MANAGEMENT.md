# Portfolio Management

## 1. Core idea: investing = managing risk, not chasing returns

* Returns come from taking risk.
* Your job is to **control risk intelligently**, not guess winners. 

## 2. Asset allocation is the main driver of results

* Asset allocation = how you split money across things like stocks, bonds, cash, countries.
* It explains **~90% of how your portfolio behaves over time**. 
* Picking individual stocks matters far less than how you allocate overall.

👉 Translation:

> Your structure matters more than your stock picks.

## 3. The 4-step investing process (simple loop)

1. **Set a policy (rules + goals)**
2. **Understand the environment (economy, markets)**
3. **Build the portfolio**
4. **Monitor and adjust continuously** 

👉 This never ends. Investing is a system, not a one-time decision.

## 4. Policy statement = your investing blueprint

This is the most underrated step.

It defines:

* Your goals (growth, income, preservation)
* Your risk tolerance
* Your constraints (time, liquidity, taxes)

Why it matters:

* Stops emotional decisions
* Keeps you disciplined
* Lets you measure performance properly 

👉 Without this, you’re just reacting to the market.

## 5. Your strategy should change over your life

### Early life (accumulation)

* Take more risk
* Focus on growth
* Time is your advantage

### Mid life (consolidation)

* Balance growth + protection
* Still long-term, but more cautious

### Retirement (spending)

* Protect capital
* Generate income
* Still need some growth (inflation)

### Later (gifting)

* Transfer wealth, estate planning 

👉 Key idea:

> Risk tolerance drops as your ability to recover drops.

## 6. Before investing: build your foundation

Don’t skip this.

* Insurance (life, health, etc.)
* Emergency fund (~6 months expenses)

Why:

* Prevents forced selling at bad times
* Protects your long-term plan 

## 7. Time horizon drives everything

* Long-term → can take more risk
* Short-term → need safety + liquidity

👉 Simple rule:

> The shorter your timeline, the less risk you can afford.

## 8. Inflation and taxes quietly destroy returns

Example from the chapter:

* Stocks: ~7.7% raw return
* After tax + inflation: ~2.9% real return 

👉 Reality:

> Your “real” returns are much lower than they look.

## 9. Stocks vs bonds vs cash (big picture)

* Stocks → higher return, higher volatility
* Bonds → moderate
* Cash → safe but weak long-term

👉 Important insight:

> Cash can be “safe” short-term but risky long-term (loses to inflation).

## 10. Diversification is non-negotiable

* Combine assets that behave differently
* Reduces risk without killing returns

👉 Even better:

* **Global diversification** (different countries)

Why:

* More opportunities
* Different economic cycles
* Lower correlation → smoother returns 

## 11. Biggest mistakes people make

* No plan at all
* Too conservative or too concentrated
* Overtrading
* Selling winners too early, holding losers too long
* Not saving enough 

👉 Brutal truth:

> Most investors lose money from behavior, not markets.
