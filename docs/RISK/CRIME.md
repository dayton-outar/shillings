# Chapter 27: Crime Insurance and Surety Bonds

**THE ISO COMMERCIAL CRIME INSURANCE PROGRAM**

There are seven basic crime coverage forms and policies in the ISO commercial crime insurance program:

- Commercial Crime Coverage Form (discovery and loss-sustained versions)
- Commercial Crime Policy (discovery and loss-sustained versions)
- Government Crime Coverage Form (discovery and loss-sustained versions)
- Government Crime Policy (discovery and loss-sustained versions)
- Employee Theft and Forgery Policy (discovery and loss-sustained versions)
- Government Employee Theft and Forgery Policy (discovery and loss-sustained versions)
- Kidnap/Ransom and Extortion Coverage

The first six can be written in two versions. The **discovery version** covers losses discovered during the policy period or within 60 days after it ends, even if the loss happened before the policy started. The **loss-sustained version** covers losses that occur during the policy period and are discovered during the policy period or within one year after it ends. It also covers losses that would have been covered under a prior policy if coverage had continued.

The commercial crime forms and policies are designed for most private businesses and nonprofits (except financial institutions). Government versions are for public entities. The kidnap/ransom and extortion coverage can be added to a package policy or bought separately.

**COMMERCIAL CRIME COVERAGE FORM (LOSS-SUSTAINED FORM)**

This form can be added to a business package policy or bought as a standalone policy to protect against crime losses like robbery, burglary, and employee theft.

### Basic Definitions

- **Robbery**: Unlawful taking of property from a person by someone who threatens or causes bodily harm, or commits an obvious unlawful act that the person witnesses.
- **Burglary**: Unlawful entry or exit with visible signs of forced entry, taking property from inside the premises.
- **Safe Burglary**: Forcible entry into a locked safe or vault (with visible marks on the outside), or taking the entire safe.
- **Theft**: Broad term for any unlawful taking of property, including robbery, burglary, shoplifting, employee theft, and forgery.

### Insuring Agreements

Businesses can choose one or more of these coverages:

- **Employee Theft**: Covers loss of money, securities, or other property stolen by employees, even if the employee isn't identified or works with others. Covers all employees on a blanket basis (or specific ones by name/position if scheduled).
- **Forgery or Alteration**: Covers losses from forged or altered checks, drafts, or similar instruments made by the business.
- **Inside the Premises—Theft of Money and Securities**: Covers theft, disappearance, or destruction of money and securities inside the premises or banking premises. Also covers damage to the premises or containers from theft attempts.
- **Inside the Premises—Robbery or Safe Burglary of Other Property**: Covers robbery of a custodian or safe burglary of property (other than money/securities) inside the premises. Property must be in a locked safe for burglary coverage.
- **Outside the Premises**: Covers theft, disappearance, or destruction of money and securities while in the care of a messenger or armored car service. Also covers robbery of other property in a messenger’s care.
- **Computer and Funds Transfer Fraud**: Covers losses from computer hackers fraudulently transferring money or property, or fraudulent instructions to banks.
- **Money Orders and Counterfeit Paper Currency**: Covers losses from accepting fake money orders or counterfeit currency in good faith during business.

> **Crime Prevention Tips for Small Businesses**  
> **Theft Prevention**  
> • Place high-value items under lock and key; use mirrors, cameras, and security tags.  
> • Train employees to spot shoplifters.  
> • Prosecute shoplifters.  
> • Do thorough background checks on hires.  
> • Mark equipment and keep good inventory records.  
> 
> **Burglary Prevention**  
> • Secure all doors, windows, and entry points with strong locks.  
> • Use good lighting and anchor safes.  
> • Check hiding spots before closing.  
> 
> **Robbery Prevention**  
> • Limit cash on hand and use drop safes.  
> • Install silent alarms and cameras.  
> • Vary bank deposit times and routes.  
> 
> **Vandalism Prevention**  
> • Keep the area well-lit and clean up damage quickly.  
> • Use landscaping to deter vandals.

**Exclusions**  
Common exclusions include:
- Dishonest acts by the business owners or partners.
- Employee theft if the business knew about prior dishonesty before the policy started.
- Losses from confidential information leaks or data breaches.
- Indirect losses (like lost business income).
- Inventory shortages based only on calculations (without direct evidence).
- Trading losses (unless endorsed).

The 2015 updates added a virtual currency exclusion (with optional endorsements to add coverage) and a fraudulent impersonation endorsement for losses from imposters tricking employees.

> **ISO’s Crime Changes: Keeping Pace with our Digital World**  
> Modern updates address virtual (crypto) currency and fraudulent impersonation via email or other tricks. Endorsements can add coverage for these new risks.

### Policy Conditions

- **Discovery Form**: Covers losses discovered during the policy period (or shortly after), even if they happened earlier.
- **Loss-Sustained Form**: Covers only losses that both occur and are discovered during the covered time.
- **Loss Sustained During Prior Insurance**: Helps when switching insurers without gaps in coverage.
- **Termination as to Any Employee**: Coverage for a specific employee ends once the business learns of their dishonesty.

**FINANCIAL INSTITUTION BONDS**

Banks and other financial institutions face big crime risks like robberies, employee theft, and forgery. They use specialized **financial institution bonds** (essentially insurance policies) for protection.

A common one is the Financial Institution Bond, Standard Form No. 24. Key insuring agreements include:

- **Fidelity**: Covers dishonest or fraudulent acts by employees.
- **On Premises**: Covers robbery, burglary, theft, disappearance, etc., on the premises.
- **In Transit**: Covers similar losses while money or securities are being transported by messengers or armored cars.
- **Forgery or Alteration** (optional): Covers forged or altered checks and instruments.
- **Securities** (optional): Covers losses from forged, stolen, or altered securities accepted in good faith.
- **Counterfeit Currency**: Covers fake money accepted by the institution.
- **Fraudulent Mortgages** (optional): Covers losses from mortgages with forged signatures.

**SURETY BONDS**

A **surety bond** is a three-party guarantee where the surety (usually an insurance company) promises to pay the obligee if the principal fails to fulfill their obligations.

### Parties to a Surety Bond

- **Principal**: The person or company that must perform the work or duty.
- **Obligee**: The party that requires the bond and receives payment if things go wrong.
- **Surety (Obligor)**: The company that issues the bond and pays if the principal defaults.

### Comparison of Surety Bonds and Insurance

- Insurance has two parties; surety bonds have three.
- Insurers expect some losses; sureties ideally expect none (premium is like a service fee).
- Insurers usually can’t recover from the insured; sureties can recover from the principal.
- Insurance covers unintentional losses; surety bonds guarantee the principal’s honesty and ability to perform.

### Types of Surety Bonds

- **Contract Bonds** (for construction):
  - Bid bond: Ensures the winner will sign the contract.
  - Performance bond: Ensures the project is completed as agreed.
  - Payment bond: Ensures workers and suppliers get paid.
  - Maintenance bond: Guarantees fixes for poor workmanship.
  - Completion bond: Protects lenders on financed projects.

- **License and Permit Bonds**: Required for businesses needing licenses (e.g., contractors, plumbers).
- **Public Official Bonds**: Protect against dishonesty by government officials.
- **Judicial Bonds**:
  - Fiduciary bond: For executors, guardians, etc., handling others’ money.
  - Court bond: For court-related guarantees.
- **Miscellaneous Surety Bonds**: For various other needs.
